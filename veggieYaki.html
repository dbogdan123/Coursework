Veggie Yaki Udon



An amazing, delicious and vegetarian dish that is full of vitamins and other nutrients. This dish is not only recommended to vegetarians, as this dish provides enough energy for a tough workout, and mixes up your healthy diet.


Pak choi: Pak chois are full of Vitamin C and E, folate and selenium. While Pak choi is full of healthy nutrients, it also boosts the immune system.

Garlic: Garlic is full of Vitamin C and B6 and magnasse. Garlic can protect against illness, can reduce blood pressure and improve cholesterol levels.

Onions: Onions help the immune system to fight off sickness and harmful bacteria, which makes this meal suitable to allow you to workout even when you're feeling sick. Onions are great source of Vitamin C, Vitamin B and potassium.


The Recipe


Ingredients

  • 1½ tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 red onion, cut into thin wedges
  • 160g mangetout
  • 70g baby corn, halved
  • 2 baby pak choi, quartered
  • 3 spring onions, sliced
  • 1 large garlic clove, crushed
  • ½ tbsp mild curry powder, or use 1 tsp garam masala
  • 4 tsp low-salt soy sauce
  • 300g ready-to-cook udon noodles
  • 1 tbsp pickled sushi ginger, chopped, plus 2 tbsp of the brine, optional

Instructions

  1. Heat the oil in a non-stick frying pan or wok over a high heat. Add the onion and fry for 5 mins. Stir in the mangetout, corn, pak choi and spring onions and cook for 5 mins more. Add the garlic, curry powder and soy sauce, and cook for another minute.
  2. Add the udon noodles along with the ginger (if using) and reserved brine, and stir in 2-3 tbsp hot water until the noodles are heated through. Divide between bowls and serve.
